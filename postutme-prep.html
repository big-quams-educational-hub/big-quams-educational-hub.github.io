University of Ibadan (UI)

UI does NOT conduct a Post UTME exam. Admission is based purely on JAMB score and O'level results — making your JAMB score extremely critical.

❌ No Post UTME Exam 📊 60% JAMB · 40% O'level 🎯 Cut-off: 200+ (Medicine: 280+)
ℹ️

How UI Admission Works

📌 UI Formula

UI uses: JAMB Score × 60% + O'level Score × 40%

O'level score is calculated based on your number of credits, especially in relevant subjects. A1 = 6 points, B2 = 5, B3 = 4, C4 = 3, C5 = 2, C6 = 1.

🎯

What to Focus On

Maximize your JAMB score Retake WAEC if grades are poor A1s in core subjects = big advantage Monitor UI CAPS portal regularly Apply for correct course
💡

UI Strategy

📌 Tips

  • Since there's no Post UTME, every JAMB mark matters — aim for 280+ for competitive courses
  • Your O'level must include the exact subjects for your course
  • Check JAMB CAPS frequently — UI offers come in batches
  • Chat with us to calculate your exact UI aggregate before applying

University of Benin (UNIBEN)

UNIBEN's Post UTME is conducted on paper or CBT. It uses a 3-component aggregate: JAMB, Post UTME, and O'level.

📝 CBT / Paper ⏱️ 45–60 Minutes 📊 50% JAMB · 25% PUTME · 25% O'level
📋

Exam Structure

SubjectQuestions
Use of English20
2–3 course subjects40
Total60

⚡ O'level Counts!

Unlike most schools, UNIBEN includes O'level in the aggregate (25%). Strong WAEC grades give you a significant edge here.

🔥

Hot Topics

English Comprehension Electrochemistry Mechanics & Electricity Organic Chemistry Calculus Ecology & Cell Biology Macro/Microeconomics Nigerian History
💡

UNIBEN Strategy

📌 Tips

  • Your O'level grade is 25% of your aggregate — retaking WAEC for better grades is worth it
  • Focus on your course subjects: they carry the most marks
  • English comprehension speed is key — practice reading fast
  • UNIBEN past questions repeat frequently — get 3–5 years
📊

UNIBEN Aggregate Calculation

📐 Formula

JAMB (÷8 = out of 50) + Post UTME (÷4 = out of 25) + O'level (÷max × 25)

Example: JAMB 260 → 32.5 + PUTME 65 → 16.25 + O'level 5A1s → ~20 = ~69/100

General Post UTME Tips

📅
Start Preparing Early
Begin Post UTME prep immediately after JAMB results. Most screenings happen 4–8 weeks after UTME. Don't wait.
📖
Get Past Questions
Your school's past Post UTME questions are the most accurate representation of what to expect. Get 3–5 years minimum.
⏱️
Time Yourself Always
Most Post UTMEs allow ~30–60 seconds per question. Practice under timed conditions every single day.
🖥️
Practice CBT
If your school uses CBT, practice online. The interface matters — fumbling with navigation wastes precious time on exam day.
📰
Read Current Affairs
Many schools include current affairs. Read Nigerian news daily for at least 3 weeks before your exam.
🏫
Know Your School
Learn founding year, location, motto, notable alumni, and key facts about your target university — they appear in questions.
😴
Sleep Before the Exam
Sleep at least 7 hours the night before. Cramming on exam night hurts performance. Your brain needs rest to recall.
🎽
Dress & Arrive Early
Arrive at least 45 minutes early. Bring your JAMB slip, a valid ID, and your admission letter. Dress decently — some schools check.
✏️
Answer All Questions
Most Nigerian Post UTMEs have no negative marking. Never leave a question blank — guess if you have to.
Study Plan

4-Week Preparation Timeline

Week 1
Gather & Diagnose

Get your school's past questions (3–5 years). Do a diagnostic test without preparation. Identify your weakest subjects and topics. Register for CBT practice.

Week 2
Subject Mastery

Focus on your two weakest subjects. Revise core JAMB topics in those subjects. Do 50 past questions per subject daily. Start reading newspapers for current affairs.

Week 3
Timed Practice

Do full mock Post UTME tests under exam conditions. Time every session. Review every wrong answer. Learn school-specific facts (history, founding, notable achievements).

Week 4 (Final)
Polish & Rest

Light revision only — focus on your strongest areas to boost confidence. Confirm your exam date, venue, and required documents. Sleep well the night before. You've prepared — trust your work.

Common Questions

Post UTME FAQs

What documents do I need for Post UTME registration?
Typically: JAMB registration slip, O'level result (WAEC/NECO), recent passport photograph, and a valid ID (NIN slip or school ID). Some schools also require your admission form receipt.
Can I write Post UTME for two schools?
Yes — but only if the exam dates don't clash. However, you can only accept one admission offer on JAMB CAPS. It's allowed to screen at multiple schools and then choose the best offer.
What happens if I fail my Post UTME?
You won't be offered admission for that session. Options include: applying to another school with lower cut-off, trying JUPEB or pre-degree programmes, retaking JAMB next year, or applying for Direct Entry if you have relevant qualifications.
Is Post UTME the same as JAMB CAPS?
No — they are different. Post UTME is an exam you write at the school. JAMB CAPS is the portal where you accept or reject an admission offer. You must write Post UTME first, then if you score well, the school sends an offer to your CAPS.
How soon are Post UTME results released?
It varies by school. Some schools (like UNILORIN) release results within 48 hours. Others take 2–4 weeks. Check your school's student portal or official website regularly after writing.
