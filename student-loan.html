Overview

What is NELFUND?

Nigerian Education Loan Fund (NELFUND)

NELFUND is a Federal Government initiative that provides interest-free loans to Nigerian students in public tertiary institutions. It covers tuition fees and a monthly upkeep stipend. The loan is repaid after graduation once you start working — not while you're in school.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility Requirements

✅

You May Qualify If:

  • You are a Nigerian citizen
  • You are enrolled in a public tertiary institution (federal or state university, polytechnic, or college of education)
  • Your institution is listed on the NELFUND approved institutions list
  • You are a full-time student
  • You do not already have another government scholarship covering your tuition
❌

You May NOT Qualify If:

  • You are enrolled in a private university or institution
  • You are on a government scholarship that covers tuition (e.g. BEA Scholarship)
  • You are not a full-time student
  • Your institution is not on the NELFUND approved list
  • You have outstanding loan defaults from previous government programmes
Coverage

What the Loan Covers

🏫

Tuition Fee Loan

Covers your school fees/tuition directly. NELFUND pays your institution on your behalf. You do not receive this money in cash — it goes straight to your school.

💵

Upkeep Stipend

A monthly living allowance paid directly to you. This is meant to help cover basic expenses like feeding, transportation and study materials while you are in school.

⚠️

The exact amounts for tuition coverage and monthly stipend are set by NELFUND and may vary by institution and year. Check nelfund.gov.ng for the current figures before applying.

Application

How to Apply — Step by Step

1

Verify Your Institution is Eligible

Before doing anything else, confirm your school is on the NELFUND approved list. Visit nelfund.gov.ng and check the list of participating institutions. Private universities are generally not included.

2

Create an Account on the NELFUND Portal

Go to nelfund.gov.ng and register with your personal details. You will need a valid email address and phone number. Keep your login details safe.

3

Gather Your Required Documents

Before filling the application, prepare everything you'll need (see the document checklist below). Having them ready will make the application smoother.

4

Complete the Application Form

Fill in your personal information, institution details, course of study, level, and bank account details. Ensure every field is accurate — mistakes can delay your application.

5

Upload Supporting Documents

Upload clear scans or photos of all required documents. Make sure they are legible and not expired. Blurry or incomplete documents will cause rejection.

6

Submit and Wait for Approval

After submitting, NELFUND will review your application. You can track your application status on the portal. Approval time varies — check the portal regularly and ensure your contact details are correct so you can be reached.

7

Disbursement

Once approved, tuition payment goes directly to your institution. The upkeep stipend is paid to your bank account. You should receive notification via email or SMS.

Repayment

How Repayment Works

📅

When Do You Start Repaying?

Repayment does not start while you are in school. It begins after you graduate and start earning income. The Federal Government has set a grace period after graduation before repayment kicks in.

The exact repayment start date and conditions are managed by NELFUND — check their portal for the current repayment policy.

💳

How is Repayment Made?

Repayment is designed to be deducted from your salary at a percentage set by the government — similar to how PAYE (Pay As You Earn) tax works. Your employer will be notified.

There is zero interest on the loan — you only repay exactly what you borrowed.

⚠️

Repayment terms are set by the Federal Government and may be adjusted. Always check nelfund.gov.ng for the most current repayment schedule and conditions.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can students in private universities apply?
Currently, NELFUND is designed for students in public (federal and state) tertiary institutions. Private university students are generally not eligible. Check the NELFUND portal for the current approved institutions list, as this may change in future.
Is the application free?
Yes, applying for the NELFUND loan is completely free. If anyone asks you to pay a fee to process or facilitate your NELFUND application, it is a scam. Only apply through the official website: nelfund.gov.ng.
Can I apply if I already have a scholarship?
If your scholarship already covers your tuition in full, you may not qualify for the tuition component. However, you may still be eligible for the upkeep stipend. Check the current eligibility rules on the NELFUND portal for the most accurate guidance.
What if I drop out or don't complete my degree?
If you leave school without completing your programme, you will still be expected to repay the amount disbursed. The repayment terms in this situation are defined by NELFUND — check their terms and conditions carefully.
How long does approval take?
Approval timelines vary. It depends on the volume of applications and whether your documents are complete and correct. Track your status on the portal and ensure your contact details are up to date so NELFUND can reach you if more information is needed.
Is my personal information safe?
NELFUND is a Federal Government agency. Only submit your information on the official nelfund.gov.ng website. Be cautious of unofficial WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels or third-party websites claiming to offer NELFUND application services.
