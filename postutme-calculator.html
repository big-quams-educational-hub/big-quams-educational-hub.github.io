    📌 How Aggregates Work

    • Most Nigerian universities combine JAMB, Post UTME, and O'level scores into one aggregate.
    • Each school has its own weighting formula — the same scores can give different aggregates at different schools.
    • Some schools (like UI) don't use Post UTME but still combine JAMB and O'level.
    • A higher aggregate = better chance of being offered admission.
    • Cut-off marks vary by course — Medicine typically needs 70+, Arts/Education may need 50+.

    🎯 Typical Cut-off Ranges

    • Medicine/Pharmacy: 70–80+ aggregate
    • Engineering/Tech: 60–75+ aggregate
    • Sciences: 55–70+ aggregate
    • Law: 60–75+ aggregate
    • Social Sciences: 50–65+ aggregate
    • Arts/Education: 45–60+ aggregate
