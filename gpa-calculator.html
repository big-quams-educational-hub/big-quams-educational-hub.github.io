How GPA is Calculated

GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Units

Quality Points = Credit Units × Grade Point

Example: If you took a 3-unit course and scored a B (4.0 on 5.0 scale):
Quality Points = 3 × 4.0 = 12.0

💡

Common Terms Explained

  • GPA — Grade Point Average for one semester
  • CGPA — Cumulative GPA across all semesters
  • Credit Unit — The weight assigned to a course (usually 1–4)
  • Carry-over — A failed course you must retake
  • Probation — Academic warning when CGPA falls below your school's minimum (usually 1.0–1.5)
